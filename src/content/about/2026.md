Hi there. I'm a husband and father of two living in [Philadelphia, PA](https://www.visitphilly.com/). I end up spending a lot of time working on the computer, but try to balance it out with family, sporting events, and long chats with friends when [AFK](http://www.urbandictionary.com/define.php?term=away%20from%20keyboard). In recent years, some of my favorite hours have gone to building elaborate LEGO sets — solo when I need quiet, or with my daughter when she'll have me — and playing Wiffle ball in the park with my son. Bits of the old me shine through every so often, mostly through these kids.

I'm an east-coaster at heart, having been born outside of Philadelphia, growing up in New Jersey, and attending [college in Delaware](https://www.udel.edu/). I went west to San Francisco at the start of my career, but found my way back to the [City of Brotherly Love](http://hiddencityphila.org/2013/11/behind-philadelphia-maneto-dissecting-the-city-seal/) in due time.

Professionally, I've made my way through companies at just about every stage — early-stage teams figuring it out in real time, growth-stage orgs trying to scale faster than their processes can keep up, and established organizations with actual org charts and opinions about font usage in slide decks. Along the way I've run my own software consultancy, helped found a tech startup, and picked up a lot of strong opinions about meetings that could have been emails.

Outside of work, I've coached youth soccer with enthusiasm that has not always been matched by results. I twice hosted an elaborate olympiad of wacky sports I invented with my friends, which I consider one of my finer contributions to society.

Personally, I married up in terms of brains and beauty, and created two amazing small humans who keep me on my toes most days. I'm slowly working through every Major League Baseball stadium — ten down, twenty to go — and have become very good at convincing my family that road trips to mid-sized American cities are a vacation. If you need me, you can probably find me kicking around my neighborhood, working toward regular-customer status at my favorite sandwich and coffee shops.
